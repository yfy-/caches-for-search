\begin{abstract}
  Past frequency statistics of queries is among the key signals to
  decide on the content of the search engine result caches. In this
  paper, we first investigate the impact of the sample size for
  frequency statistics for most popular cache eviction strategies in
  the literature, and show that cache performance improves with larger
  samples, i.e., by storing the frequencies of all (or, most of) the
  queries seen by the search engine. Secondly, we show that a recently
  proposed approach based on Counting Bloom Filters serve well to
  store the query frequency statistics in a compact a manner, while
  still providing comparable cache performance to keeping all
  frequencies in a raw manner.
\end{abstract}
